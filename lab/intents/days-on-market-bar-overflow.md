# Days-on-market bar grows past the end of its chart

On a property's record page, the "Days on Market" component draws a colored bar showing how
long the property has been listed, on a scale that tops out at 90 days. For properties that
have been on the market longer than 90 days — exactly the ones a broker most needs to talk
about — the bar keeps growing past the end of the chart and pokes out of the component
instead of stopping at the maximum.

The bar should never extend beyond the chart's end: anything at 90 days or more shows a full
bar. The number next to the bar should keep showing the real days on market, however large.
